Computes tendency term for horizontal momentum mixing with Leith parameterization.

Author
Mark Petersen, Todd Ringler
Date
22 October 2012

This routine computes the horizontal mixing tendency for momentum based on the Leith closure. The Leith closure is the enstrophy-cascade analogy to the Smagorinsky (1963) energy-cascade closure, i.e. Leith (1996) assumes an inertial range of enstrophy flux moving toward the mesh scale. The assumption of an enstrophy cascade and dimensional analysis produces right-hand-side dissipation, $\bf{D}$, of velocity of the form $ {\bf D} = \nabla \cdot \left( \nu_\ast \nabla {\bf u} \right) = \nabla \cdot \left( \gamma \left| \nabla \omega \right| \left( \Delta x \right)^3 \nabla \bf{u} \right) where $\omega$ is the relative vorticity and $\gamma$ is a non-dimensional, $O(1)$ parameter. We set $\gamma=1$.

[in]relativevorticityInput: relative vorticity

[in,out]viscosityInput: viscosity
[out]errOutput: error flag
