18!for WRFv3.4 and WRFv3.4.1:

20! BOTH LAND AND WATER:
21!1) Calculation of stability parameter (z/L) taken from Li et al. (2010 BLM)
22! for first iteration of first time step; afterwards, exact calculation.
23!2) Fixed isflux=0 option to turn off scalar fluxes, but keep momentum
24! fluxes for idealized studies (credit: Anna Fitch).
25!3) Kinematic viscosity now varies with temperature
26!4) Uses Monin-Obukhov flux-profile relationships more consistent with
27! those used in the MYNN PBL code.
28!5) Allows negative QFX, similar to MYJ scheme

30! LAND only:
31!1) iz0tlnd option is now available with the following options:
32! (default) =0: Zilitinkevich (1995)
33! =1: Czil_new (modified according to Chen & Zhang 2008)
34! =2: Modified Yang et al (2002, 2008) - generalized for all landuse
35! =3: constant zt = z0/7.4 (original form; Garratt 1992)
36! =4: Pan et al. (1994) with RUC mods for z_q, zili for z_t
37!2) Relaxed u* minimum from 0.1 to 0.01
38!
39! WATER only:
40!1) isftcflx option is now available with the following options:
41! (default) =0: z0, zt, and zq from COARE3.0 (Fairall et al 2003)
42! =1: z0 from Davis et al (2008), zt & zq from COARE3.0
43! =2: z0 from Davis et al (2008), zt & zq from Garratt (1992)
44! =3: z0 from Taylor and Yelland (2004), zt and zq from COARE3.0
45! =4: z0 from Zilitinkevich (2001), zt & zq from COARE3.0
46!
47! SNOW/ICE only:
48!1) Added Andreas (2002) snow/ice parameterization for thermal and
49! moisture roughness to help reduce the cool/moist bias in the arctic
50! region.
51!
52!NOTE: This code was primarily tested in combination with the RUC LSM.
53! Performance with the Noah (or other) LSM is relatively unknown.

1447 SUBROUTINE fairall_2001(Zt,Zq,Ren,ustar,visc)
1448
1449 !This formulation for thermal and moisture roughness length (Zt and Zq)

1453 !relationship overestimated roughness lengths for low Reynolds number
1454 !flows, so a smooth flow relationship, taken from Garrattt (1992, p. 102),
1455 !is used for flows with Ren < 2.
1456 !
1457 !Note that this formulation should not be used with the Davis et al.
1458 !(2008) formulation for Zo, because that formulation produces much
1459 !smaller u* (Ren), resulting in a large Zt and Zq. It works best with
1460 !the Charnock or the Taylor and Yelland relationships.
1461 !
1462 !This is for use over water only.
1463

1466 REAL, INTENT(OUT) :: Zt,Zq
1467
1468 IF (ren .le. 2.) then
1469
1470 zt = (5.5e-5)*(ren**(-0.60))
1471 zq = zt
1472 !FOR SMOOTH SEAS, USE GARRATT
1473 !Zq = 0.2*visc/MAX(ustar,0.1)
1474 !Zq = 0.3*visc/MAX(ustar,0.1)
1475
1476 ELSE
